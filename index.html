+ Investment Philosophy

We deploy capital only when the expected return materially exceeds our required rate of return and that of any existing holding it would displace. Every opportunity is evaluated through a proprietary expected return framework assessing the operating cash flow yield at the prevailing price, the quality and sustainability of growth, and the conservatism embedded in the current market valuation.

We target businesses with durable and widening competitive advantages, high returns on tangible capital, and management teams whose capital allocation decisions compound per-share value over a decade or longer. We assess competitive position through a structured evaluation of moat strength and trajectory, and management quality through the discipline with which capital is allocated among reinvestment, acquisitions, share repurchases, dividends, and balance sheet management.

We distinguish between organic growth and growth dependent on the returns generated by incrementally deployed capital. We balance current and future cash earnings when assessing valuation, seeking a reasonable price for durable quality rather than overpaying for growth or underpaying for value traps.

Our structural advantage is duration — a capital structure permitting holding periods of ten to fifteen years or longer, capturing compound growth that time-constrained vehicles must forfeit. Partners co-invest alongside our investors, ensuring alignment of interests.

+ Investment Strategy & Process

The Fund pursues a concentrated investment strategy across public and private markets, with allocations driven by share class risk and liquidity profiles.

Public Securities: The Fund invests in publicly-traded securities globally, targeting businesses with the quality and expected return characteristics described above. We employ a patient, low-turnover approach with intended holding periods exceeding five years.

Private Investments: The Fund pursues private investments across the capital structure, including minority, significant minority, and controlling positions. We seek businesses where we can contribute active operational value through board representation, capital structure optimisation, or strategic advisory. Investments may include established cash-generative businesses as well as businesses at earlier stages of development where we have conviction in the trajectory of future cash flows. Private opportunities are evaluated using the same expected return framework applied to public securities. Sourcing derives from proprietary relationships developed through our principals' combined thirty years of transaction experience.

Geographic Focus: The Fund may invest globally. The Investment Advisor specialises in the United States, Europe, and the Gulf Cooperation Council region.

Decision Process: All investments require unanimous Investment Committee approval.

+ Risk Management Framework

Our multi-layered risk management approach is designed to preserve capital while capturing asymmetric upside opportunities.

Market Risk Management: We employ daily monitoring, stress testing across macroeconomic and idiosyncratic scenarios, correlation analysis, and active drawdown controls to manage portfolio-level exposure.

Concentration Discipline: Portfolio construction is governed by position-sizing limits, sector concentration constraints, and geographic diversification requirements to mitigate undue risk concentration.

ESG Integration: ESG factors are systematically evaluated in every investment decision. We maintain a firm-wide exclusion policy for weapons, tobacco, and other harmful industries.

Risk Factors: Investing involves substantial risk including possible loss of principal. Private investments are illiquid. Currency risk exists in international investments. Leverage may be employed. Past performance does not guarantee future results.
